Experience, Research & Publications

  1.              

    DOPLAX: A Novel Approach for Battery Health Estimation Using a Deep Operator Physics-Informed Neural Network and Hopf–Lax Optimization

                  March–Oct 2025

    Research conducted under Prof. Babak Khalaj, Prof. Farzan Farnia, and Prof. Mohammad Hossein Rohban. We developed advanced Physics-Informed Neural Networks for estimating battery State of Health (SOH). Two architectures were proposed: one framed battery lifetime estimation as a Hopf–Lax optimization problem to solve the governing differential equations, and the other used a DeepONet backbone as a complementary approach. Combining these models, we designed a third network with superior performance. The project is complete, and the manuscript is being prepared for submission to IEEE Transactions on Industrial Informatics. Our methods outperformed all prior works on four datasets (XJTU, TJU, MIT, HUST), representing a significant contribution to battery lifetime prediction grounded in rigorous mathematical optimization. Relevant papers:

    On some neural network architectures that can represent viscosity solutions of certain high dimensional Hamilton–Jacobi partial differential equations
    Physics-informed neural network for lithiumion battery degradation stablemodeling and prognosis

               

  2. Research on Distributed Sparse Learning with Bounded Communication Cost

    Sep 2024 – March 2025

    Research conducted under Prof. Amir Najafi. I focused on distributed sparse learning in a star-shaped network within a decentralized regime. The goal was to identify influential features from users while decreasing the total communication cost to increase user privacy in real-world conditions. In many scenarios, data could not be gathered at a single node, making the decentralized regime crucial. The most relevant paper to my research was:

    Distributed Sparse Feature Selection in Communication-Restricted Networks
